facts about Kashmir

The Kashmir region is part of the larger Jammu and Kashmir territory, bordered by India, Pakistan, and China. It includes the Kashmir Valley, Jammu, and Ladakh regions. The valley is often referred to as "heaven on Earth" due to its breathtaking scenery.Kashmir was an independent princely state under British India and joined India in 1947 after the partition, sparking disputes between India and Pakistan. The region has been a flashpoint for conflict, with three wars fought between India and Pakistan and ongoing territorial disputes involving China. Natural Beauty

Kashmir is known for its lush green valleys, snow-capped mountains, rivers, and serene Dal and Wular Lakes. Popular destinations include Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonmarg.The region boasts a rich cultural heritage, blending influences from Central Asia, South Asia, and Persian traditions. Kashmiri cuisine, like Rogan Josh and Kahwa (traditional tea), is renowned for its flavors. Kashmiri shawls and carpets are globally admired for their intricate craftsmanshipArticle 370 of the Indian Constitution granted Jammu and Kashmir special autonomy until its abrogation in August 2019. The region was then reorganized into two union territories: Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh..
